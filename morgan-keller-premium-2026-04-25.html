The five findings that matter most

  1. Page coverage is the single biggest lever. Morgan-Keller ranks #1 or #2 organic for the queries it has dedicated content for, and falls out of the top 10 for queries it doesn't. Building 3-4 specialty service pages (medical office, healthcare facility, tenant fit-out, warehouse) would meaningfully expand visibility without changing anything about the existing pages.
  2. Schema markup is a clean win — no competitor uses it. Morgan-Keller, Kinsley, Harkins, and KasCon all return zero JSON-LD schema across their service pages. Deploying the schema bundle makes Morgan-Keller eligible for FAQ rich cards, AI Overview citations, and Knowledge Panel data that none of the competitors are positioned for.
  3. Service-page content depth is thin. Morgan-Keller's seven service pages average 277 words of body copy each. The shortest (General Contracting, 172 words) and second-shortest (Construction Management, 180 words) are exactly the queries Morgan-Keller currently ranks for — meaning even modest content depth pays off in this market.
  4. The homepage has two mechanical fixes worth surfacing. The homepage <title> tag renders as "| Morgan Keller" — a missing variable in a template — and the homepage has an empty H1. Both are quick fixes with outsized SEO impact.
  5. Brand authority is Morgan-Keller's quietest strength. Brand Signal score is 52 — the highest of the four GCs analyzed. Trust Flow is 37 — also the highest. Spam Score is 1 — tied for cleanest. This authority profile is what allows Morgan-Keller to rank #1-2 for competitive queries despite a relatively small backlink count (1,700) compared to Kinsley (9,500). Sustaining and showcasing this authority through dedicated EEAT content (named PMs, project case studies, certifications) compounds the advantage.
Headline opportunity. Frederick County's commercial construction search market is small enough that it can be won with disciplined work — not big enough that paid budgets matter. The path to category leadership is page coverage + schema deployment + monthly project case studies. Morgan-Keller is closer to that goal than the surface metrics suggest.

The gap that matters most: H2 / H3 hierarchy on Morgan-Keller service pages

Beyond word count, the structural pattern on Morgan-Keller's service pages is unusually flat. Most pages have one H1 (good), then H2s used primarily for navigation and footer elements ("Primary Nav", "Secondary Nav", "Site Map", "Follow Us"), then no H3s at all on most service pages. This isn't a content problem — it's a semantic-HTML problem. The page template wraps nav and footer text in H2 tags, which dilutes the H2 signal Google uses to understand the page's actual subject hierarchy. Restructuring the template to use H2/H3 only for content sections (and proper <nav> elements for navigation) would meaningfully strengthen the topical signal on every page without writing new content.

Each payload targets a specific page or page-type. Drop each inside a <script type="application/ld+json">…</script> block in the page's <head>.

What's in the bundle

Ready-to-deploy payloads:

  • GeneralContractor for the homepage — includes name, founding year, all addresses, area served, knowsAbout topic graph, full service catalog, awards, memberships
  • Service for each of the 7 service pages (preconstruction, general contracting, construction management, design-build, concrete, sustainable, facilities maintenance) — each linked back to the parent organization
  • BreadcrumbList template for service pages (apply to each)
  • FAQPage for the homepage or About page — 5 questions sourced from Google's "People also ask" for related queries, with draft answers Morgan-Keller can review and adjust

Templates with placeholders for Morgan-Keller to fill in:

  • Person template for named team members (homepage already surfaces Mark Remaklus, Scott Armiger, Dennis Hunter, Joe Wasilewski as candidates)
  • CreativeWork / Place template for project case studies (8 candidate projects identified from the homepage features and news section)

Deployment priority

  1. Organization schema on the homepage — single deploy, highest impact, eligible for Knowledge Panel and Brand Mentions
  2. Service schema on each service page — 7 deploys, supports SERP service-feature eligibility
  3. BreadcrumbList on each page — sitelinks support, easy template
  4. FAQPage on the homepage or selected service page — FAQ rich card eligibility, AI Overview citation eligibility
  5. Person schema on each named-team-member page as those bio pages get built — author/expertise EEAT signal
  6. CreativeWork schema on each project case study as those /projects/ pages get built — strongest EEAT signal commercial GCs can deploy

Validation before publish

Before publishing each schema, validate via Google's Rich Results Test (search.google.com/test/rich-results) to confirm Google can parse the payload correctly. The schema.org validator (validator.schema.org) provides additional structural checks.

Why this matters more for Morgan-Keller than for most clients

Three of the GC competitors analyzed have zero schema. None of the 11 organic competitors surfaced in the SERP analysis appear to deploy structured data either. The commercial-GC niche in this market is currently unschemed, which means schema deployment is not a "catch up" move — it's a "first mover" move. Combined with Morgan-Keller's existing Brand Signal (52, highest of competitors), Trust Flow (37, highest), and active LLM visibility on three platforms, the structured data layer is the missing piece that converts existing authority into AI Overview eligibility, FAQ rich cards, and Knowledge Panel population.

Methodology notes

  • Local SEO methodology: All keyword research used city-phrase keywords ("frederick md") with county-level location filtering (Frederick County, MD). County names are not searched as queries; city names are. Uniform application across all Apollo SEO clients.
  • No US-wide data was used. US-wide search volume inflates expectations and breaks trust with local-service buyers. Every metric in this report is filtered to Frederick County, MD or the explicit state-level keyword (where indicated).
  • Honest reporting on gaps: Two keywords returned no data from the research source and are flagged as such. Backlink 30-day velocity is rendered as an interactive chart rather than a discrete number by the data source — flagged Incomplete rather than approximated. Brand Signal scores on two competitors (Harkins, KasCon) appear as 0 — likely propagation-pending since their tracking projects were created within the past 24 hours; will normalize over the coming days.
