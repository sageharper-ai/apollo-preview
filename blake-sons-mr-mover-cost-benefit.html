1. What's actually broken (and what's not)

The link on blakemoving.com that sends customers to a "Free Quote" inventory form has been broken since Tom flagged it earlier this spring. The destination — quote.mrmover.com — was discontinued when Mr. Mover was sold and dropped support for the older quote-tool integration Blake had been using. That broken link is the visible problem.

Underneath that, Mr. Mover continues to handle the rest of Blake's day-to-day operations — scheduling everyone can see, contract generation from Word templates, the customer-to-booked-job dispatch flow, the warehouse dispatch printouts Nick uses, and the time-tracking pipeline that feeds Sharon's QuickBooks payroll. None of that other stuff has stopped working. The risk is that another piece breaks next, with no support to fix it.

The clarified ask

Per Tom's direction: he doesn't want a full Mr. Mover replacement right now. He wants the inventory-form portion working again — the part that lets a customer self-serve their move details online so Shannon can turn that into a quote. Everything else Mr. Mover does, leave alone for now.

This brief is structured around that ask first. Then it lays out what a full replacement would look like, in case the next thing breaks and the conversation has to expand quickly.

3. Path A — Inventory-form-only (Tom's actual ask)

This path replaces just the inventory quote form. The customer fills it out on blakemoving.com. The completed form lands in Shannon's inbox (or whatever destination Shannon prefers) with all the inventory details captured. Shannon takes that, runs it through her quote process exactly the way she does today, and sends back the estimate. Mr. Mover stays unchanged for everything else.

Sub-options under Path A

The choice inside Path A is really about scope-over-time: do you want a tool that only does the inventory form (and nothing else, ever), or a tool that does the inventory form today and can grow into the rest of what Mr. Mover does without forcing Blake to switch vendors later? Below in priority order.

8. Apollo's recommendation

Short answer

Start with A1 (MoverBase Mover Pro plan, $29/mo). Turn on only the inventory + quote tools today. It solves Tom's exact ask in 1–2 weeks, costs ~$29/mo (likely lower than Mr. Mover was), and the rest of the modules (scheduling, contracts, dispatch, time tracking → QuickBooks) are already in the same plan, ready to switch on if anything else degrades.

The selection criterion that drove this pick: scope-over-time

Tom's actual ask is the inventory form. The instinct is to find the cheapest inventory-only tool. But "inventory-only forever" tools (Yembo, generic form builders) leave Blake exposed if anything else in Mr. Mover breaks — Blake then has to evaluate, buy, learn, and integrate a second tool.

A better selection criterion: find the cheapest tool that solves today's problem AND already includes the scale path for tomorrow's possible problems. That criterion makes A1 (MoverBase) win — at $29/mo it's competitive with even inventory-only tools, and it includes the full Mr. Mover replacement in the same plan. Blake pays once, switches things on as needed.

Movegistics (A3) meets the same scope-over-time criterion with premium AI surveys, but the demo-gated pricing creates friction Tom may not want. If price isn't a concern and AI inventory UX is, A3 is a defensible alternative — worth the demo call.

Why this beats the alternatives

  • vs. A2 (custom inventory form): A1 ships sooner, vendor handles maintenance, and the upgrade path to full Mr. Mover replacement is a vendor toggle — a custom build would need a separate Apollo project later.
  • vs. A3 (Movegistics): only loses on AI-survey UX and known pricing. Wins on transparency and lower starting cost. If Tom values the AI walkthrough enough to do a demo, A3 is reasonable.
  • vs. A4 (Yembo): Yembo's AI is best-in-class for the inventory step but has zero scale path. Blake would be locked into "inventory only forever" and would need a second tool if anything else in Mr. Mover breaks.
  • vs. SmartMoving / Elromco: over-buying. If Mr. Mover is still doing scheduling, contracts, dispatch, and payroll fine, paying $300+/mo to replace what's working is unjustified — and MoverBase has the same scale path at 1/10th the price.
  • vs. MoveHQ: same parent company that just dropped support on the older Mr. Mover quote tool. Picking them again is "trust the same vendor twice."
  • vs. C (full custom): right answer if Blake commits to a 5+ year horizon and wants zero vendor dependency. For solving the broken link this month, it's the longer road.

What we'd do next, if you say yes to A1

  1. Apollo opens MoverBase account on Blake's behalf.
  2. Shannon and Apollo populate Blake's pricing rules into MoverBase's quote engine (Shannon's time required; estimate provided after a brief scoping call).
  3. Apollo embeds the MoverBase inventory form on blakemoving.com, replacing the current broken link.
  4. Apollo configures the email-to-Shannon notification path with full inventory detail.
  5. Test pass with three sample inventories before going live.
  6. Monitor for 30 days. If something doesn't fit, Apollo swaps to A2 (custom) or A different vendor without Blake having to re-do anything customer-facing.

10. Questions Tom or Shannon need to answer first

These don't change the recommendation but they affect setup speed:

  1. Pricing rules. Do you have a documented pricing model (rate per crew, per hour, per mile, per add-on like piano or appliance disconnect)? If yes, Apollo plugs it in. If it lives in Tom's head, we capture it during setup.
  2. Customer data destination. Where do you want completed inventory forms to land? Email-to-Shannon is the default; we can also push them into Mr. Mover's intake side if the API still accepts it, or a Google Sheet, or both.
  3. Historical quotes. Do you need to search "what did we quote this customer last time?" against a year of historical data, or is "this customer is new to us" the typical case?
  4. Style. Should the inventory form match the new website design (recommended) or stay generic for speed?
  5. Approval pathway for the quote that goes back out. Today Shannon manually generates the quote after Mr. Mover. Should Path A also send the customer an automated preliminary estimate, or strictly intake-only with Shannon doing the formal quote?
