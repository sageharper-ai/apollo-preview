Bottom Line

The store cannot accept a real order today.

The inventory catalog and core marketing pages (home, financing) are in good shape. The infrastructure that converts a visitor into a paying customer — payments, shipping methods, tax rates, and a real domain — is not yet wired up.

This is normal for a store at this stage. The good news: most of the launch blockers are configuration tasks, not build tasks. A focused day or two on the items in the "Must-do" section gets the store live.

The recommendation below splits the work into three buckets so you can decide what to launch with and what to add after:

  • Must-do — blocks first transaction, legal compliance, or trust at first impression. Must ship before launch.
  • Should-do — hurts conversion or looks unfinished, but doesn't block a sale. Strongly recommended for launch.
  • Nice-to-have — uplift, polish, marketing capability. Add post-launch.

01Must-do before launch

Blocks first transaction, legal compliance, or trust at first impression.
1. Connect a real payment processor
Blocker Client decision Half-day
Status
Store is currently in test-card mode only. No live processor (BigCommerce Payments, Stripe, PayPal, Klarna) is connected.
Why it matters
Without a live processor, every customer who clicks "Buy" hits a dead end. This is the single biggest launch blocker.
Recommendation
BigCommerce Payments (preferred — bundles credit cards, PayPal, Venmo, Pay Later, and digital wallets in one checkout, no separate contracts) or Stripe (more granular control, better if Parsons already has a Stripe relationship). Need a decision from Parsons on which to set up, plus underwriting paperwork.
2. Enable free shipping (MAP-priced products)
Blocker Apollo 1 hour
Status
No shipping origin address set. The U.S. shipping zone exists but zero shipping methods are turned on (Free, Flat rate, Pick-up — all off).
Why it matters
Without at least one shipping method enabled, the checkout will reject every order. Per the agreed scope, shipping cost is baked into MAP pricing rather than calculated at checkout — but the "Free Shipping" method still needs to be turned on for the checkout to complete.
Recommendation
Set Parsons warehouse address as origin. Toggle "Free shipping" ON for the U.S. zone (already the agreed scope — shipping baked into MAP). Add an optional homepage callout: "Free shipping on every order". Defer FedEx / UPS live-rate setup unless Parsons later wants to surface dimensional pricing for oversized SKUs.
4. Confirm fulfillment routing (Parsons vs. vendor catalogs)
To-do Client decision 1 hour
Status
The kickoff brief noted that some products fulfill from Parsons directly and some come from vendor catalogs without their own fulfillment. The handoff for each product family needs confirmation before launch so orders don't sit unrouted.
Why it matters
Day-one orders need a clear answer to: "Who picks, packs, and ships this SKU?" Without a documented routing rule, fulfillment delays become customer-service problems immediately.
Recommendation
Confirm with Parsons: which categories fulfill from Parsons warehouse (label print + ship), which dropship from vendor (PO sent on order), which are special-order. A simple one-page routing map per category is enough for launch — a real fulfillment integration (Sage 100, ShipStation) is a Phase 2 conversation.
4. Set sales tax rates (or connect Avalara)
Missing Client decision 1–2 hours
Status
Basic Tax module is enabled with 4 tax classes and 1 zone, but 0 actual tax rates are configured. The 10% fallback is a default, not a policy.
Why it matters
Selling auto equipment across state lines triggers sales tax nexus rules. Charging the wrong tax (or none) creates legal exposure and reconciliation headaches at year-end. This is the cheapest item on the list to fix and the most expensive one to ignore.
Recommendation
For a starting catalog, manually configure the home-state rate plus any states with confirmed nexus. If the product mix is wider than one state, install Avalara AvaTax (BigCommerce's recommended tax service) — it auto-applies the right rate per shipping address.
5. Move store to a custom domain (shopautoequip.com)
Blocker Apollo + Client 1–3 hours + DNS propagation
Status
Storefront is currently on the BigCommerce subdomain shopautoequipcom.mybigcommerce.com. No custom domain attached.
Why it matters
Trust, branding, SEO indexability, and deliverability of order-confirmation emails all depend on a real domain. Customers will not trust a checkout on a .mybigcommerce.com URL.
Recommendation
Confirm Parsons owns shopautoequip.com (or chosen variant). Point DNS at BigCommerce, attach in admin, verify SSL provisions automatically. Add 24–48 hours of propagation buffer to the launch timeline.
6. SSL certificate provisioned + HTTPS enforced
Pending domain Apollo Automatic
Status
SSL is provisioned automatically by BigCommerce once the custom domain is attached. Verification needed after Item 4 is complete.
Why it matters
Customers will not (and should not) submit credit-card data over a non-HTTPS page. Browsers now flag non-HTTPS sites as "Not Secure" — fatal for trust at the moment of purchase.
Recommendation
Verify SSL is active and HTTPS is enforced site-wide after the domain attaches. Confirm by visiting the bare domain — should auto-redirect to https://.
7. Configure order notifications (admin + customer)
Verify Apollo 30 min
Status
Admin order alerts need a confirmed recipient at Parsons. Customer-facing transactional emails (order confirmation, shipping confirmation) need branding review and a real "from" address tied to the new domain.
Why it matters
If no one at Parsons gets pinged when an order comes in, fulfillment delays start immediately. If customer emails come from a generic noreply@ with no logo, customers think the order didn't go through.
Recommendation
Set admin recipient to the order-fulfillment owner's email at Parsons. Set customer-facing "from" to orders@shopautoequip.com. Quick brand-review pass on the email templates so they match the site.
8. Required legal pages — Privacy, Terms, Returns, Shipping policy
Verify Client + Apollo Half-day
Status
Must-have legal/policy pages need to exist and be linked from the footer before launch: Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, Return Policy, Shipping Policy. State of presence to be confirmed during the storefront walk-through.
Why it matters
Privacy Policy is a legal requirement in every U.S. state with a shopping site. Returns and shipping policies are baseline trust signals — customers check these before purchase, especially on higher-ticket auto-equipment items. Their absence is a conversion killer.
Recommendation
Parsons supplies the policy language (or signs off on a starter template Apollo drafts). Apollo publishes as BigCommerce Web Pages and links from the footer.
9. Contact information visible in header/footer
Verify Apollo 15 min
Status
Phone number, business address, and contact email need to be visible site-wide (typically header or footer). State of presence to be confirmed during the storefront walk-through.
Why it matters
B2B and high-ticket buyers want to call before they click "Buy." Missing contact info is a top-three reason for cart abandonment on equipment sites. It also affects local SEO and Google Shopping eligibility.
Recommendation
Add Parsons phone, business address, and info@shopautoequip.com to the footer at minimum. Phone in the header ("call to order") for higher-intent buyers.
10. Order confirmation flow tested end-to-end
To-do Apollo 1 hour
Status
Once payment + shipping + tax + domain are configured, a real end-to-end test order needs to be placed and refunded to verify the full flow.
Why it matters
Untested checkout = guaranteed first-day surprise. Every store launch finds at least one config issue (wrong tax zone, broken email template, shipping rate mis-applied) that only surfaces in a real transaction.
Recommendation
Place a real $1 test order using a Parsons credit card after all Must-do items are complete. Verify: order placed, admin email received, customer email received, tax calculated correctly, shipping rate applied. Then refund.
11. Complete BigCommerce store setup wizard
To-do Apollo 15 min
Status
The "Welcome to BigCommerce" onboarding wizard has not been completed. Some downstream features (recommendations, default settings) reference inputs from this wizard.
Why it matters
Quick win — clears the dashboard banner and gives BigCommerce the data it needs to surface the right recommendations and analytics defaults.
Recommendation
Walk through the wizard once Parsons confirms business category, currency, and basic preferences.

02Should-do before launch

Hurts conversion or looks unfinished, but doesn't block a sale.
12. Abandoned cart recovery emails
Verify Apollo 30 min
Why
BigCommerce has built-in abandoned-cart saver — recovers 10–15% of abandoned carts on average. For higher-ticket auto equipment that recovery rate is meaningful revenue. Free to enable, three-email sequence with discount on the third send is a sensible default.
13. Google Analytics 4 + Meta Pixel installed
Verify Apollo 30 min
Why
Without analytics installed at launch, the first month of traffic data is lost forever. GA4 for traffic + behavior. Meta Pixel for ad retargeting (mandatory if Parsons plans any Facebook/Instagram ads). Both via BigCommerce's native Data Solutions integration — no manual code.
14. Sitemap + robots.txt verified for indexing
Verify Apollo 30 min
Why
BigCommerce auto-generates both. Need to verify no noindex remnant from test mode, and submit the sitemap to Google Search Console + Bing Webmaster Tools day-of-launch so indexing starts immediately.
15. Page titles + meta descriptions across catalog
Verify Apollo + Client 1–2 days for full catalog
Why
SEO baseline. Default product titles/descriptions from a spreadsheet import are usually thin. Pass needed for: home page, financing page, top-level categories, and top 20–50 products. Remaining catalog can be templated and improved post-launch.
16. Mobile responsiveness verified across key flows
Verify Apollo 1 hour
Why
60%+ of e-commerce traffic is mobile. Walk through home → category → product → cart → checkout on a real iPhone and Android. Any layout breaks here are launch blockers in disguise.
17. Search functionality tuned
Verify Apollo 30 min
Why
Site search converts 2–3x higher than browse. Verify the BigCommerce native search returns relevant results for the top 10 queries Parsons expects (e.g., specific product names, SKUs, common categories). Add synonyms if needed.
18. 404 page + custom navigation paths
Verify Apollo 30 min
Why
404 page should match the site's branding and offer a path forward (search, top categories, contact). Default BigCommerce 404 is generic and ends the visit.
19. Account creation flow + form fields reviewed
Verify Apollo 30 min
Why
Confirm guest checkout is enabled (lower friction = higher conversion). Audit the account-signup form fields — every required field is a conversion drag. Strip to email + password + name unless there's a strong business reason for more.
20. Payment method badges + trust signals visible at checkout
To-do Apollo 30 min
Why
Visible Visa/Mastercard/Amex/PayPal logos near the cart and footer reduce checkout drop-off measurably. Add an SSL/secure-checkout badge near the credit card form. Free, fast, well-documented uplift.

03Nice-to-have post-launch

Uplift, polish, marketing capability — add after launch.
21. Customer reviews + ratings
Half-day setup
Why
Reviews lift conversion 10–30% on equipment categories. BigCommerce has native reviews; Yotpo or Judge.me add richer review widgets and email solicitation. Best added once a few real orders have shipped to seed the reviews.
22. Email marketing platform (Klaviyo / Mailchimp)
Half-day setup
Why
Captures email signups, runs welcome series, post-purchase flows, win-back campaigns. Klaviyo is the industry default for e-commerce. Worth standing up in week 2–4 once there's a list to mail.
23. Live chat / chatbot
2 hours
Why
High-ticket equipment buyers ask pre-purchase questions. Tidio, Gorgias, or Intercom are common picks. Only worth it if Parsons has someone who can respond within minutes during business hours — otherwise it hurts more than it helps.
24. Cross-sell / upsell on product pages + cart
2 hours
Why
"Frequently bought together" and cart upsells lift average order value 5–15%. BigCommerce native related products is the cheap version; apps like Justuno do it smarter. Wait until purchase data exists.
25. Promotions + coupon system
1 hour
Why
Configure first-purchase discount, free-shipping threshold, seasonal codes. BigCommerce native — no app needed. Add when there's a marketing reason (launch promo, holiday, abandoned-cart code).
26. Schema markup + rich results
Half-day
Why
Product schema unlocks Google Shopping rich results (price, availability, ratings in search). BigCommerce themes generate basic Product schema; can be extended with FAQ, Breadcrumb, Organization schema for additional SERP real estate.
27. ShipStation for fulfillment
1 hour
Why
Once order volume is past ~10–20/week, batch label printing and rate-shopping across carriers via ShipStation saves real time and money. Free for low volume; tiered after that.
28. Wishlist / save-for-later
30 min
Why
BigCommerce native — toggle on. Useful for high-ticket categories where the buyer comes back later. Low effort, modest value.
