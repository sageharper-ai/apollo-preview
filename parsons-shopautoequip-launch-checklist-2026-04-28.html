01What we need from Parsons

Decisions, content, and access we're waiting on to unblock launch work.
Underwriting + decision on payment processor
Blocker

The store cannot accept a real order until a payment processor is connected. Today only test-card processing is enabled.

What we need: Parsons to choose between BigCommerce Payments (bundles cards, PayPal, Venmo, Pay Later, digital wallets in one checkout — recommended) or Stripe (better if Parsons has an existing Stripe relationship). Underwriting paperwork lives with the business owner — bank info, EIN, business address.

Sales tax direction (manual rates or Avalara)
Pre-launch

No tax rates are configured. Selling auto equipment across state lines triggers nexus rules — getting this wrong creates legal exposure and reconciliation headaches at year-end.

What we need: Parsons' position on tax — either (a) the list of states where Parsons has sales-tax nexus and we configure manually, or (b) a green light to install Avalara AvaTax, which auto-applies the correct rate per shipping address. Pricing on Avalara depends on transaction volume.

Legal and policy content
Pre-launch

Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, Return Policy, and Shipping Policy pages need to be live and linked from the footer before launch. Privacy is a legal requirement; the others are baseline trust signals customers check before purchase.

What we need: Parsons supplies the policy text, OR signs off on starter templates Apollo drafts based on Parsons' return-window, restocking-fee, and warranty practices.

Order-fulfillment owner and notification email
Pre-launch

When an order comes in, someone at Parsons needs to be alerted so fulfillment can begin. The store needs a real "from" address tied to the new domain for customer-facing emails so confirmation messages don't look like spam.

What we need: the email address of the team member who owns order fulfillment (admin alert recipient), plus confirmation that orders@shopautoequip.com works as the customer-facing "from" address.

About Us page content + contact information
Pre-launch

The home page already has an About section with a "Read More" button — but that button isn't linked to a full page yet. We also need contact details to surface in the header and footer.

What we need: About Us page copy (or sign-off on a draft Apollo writes), plus the phone number, business address, and contact email Parsons wants displayed site-wide.

Pop-up content for first promo (AC Machines spring)
Quick

The pop-up framework was scaffolded during the homepage build. The first scheduled promo — AC Machines spring — needs final imagery and copy before it goes live.

What we need: the AC Machines promo image and rough copy from Allie. Apollo styles and wires the CTA to the right category page.

03Open questions we need answered

Quick decisions Parsons makes that unblock multiple Apollo tasks above.

Which payment processor — BigCommerce Payments or Stripe?

BC Payments is the simpler all-in-one (cards, PayPal, Venmo, Pay Later, wallets, no separate contracts). Stripe is more granular and worth choosing if Parsons already has a Stripe account and processing relationship.

Manual sales-tax setup or Avalara?

If Parsons has only one or two states of nexus, manual rates in Basic Tax are free and fine. If the catalog will sell into many states or scale fast, Avalara handles it automatically — there's a monthly cost based on transaction volume.

Returns policy specifics — timeframe, restocking, who pays return shipping?

Apollo can draft a starter Return Policy, but we need Parsons' actual practice on (a) return window in days, (b) any restocking fees, (c) which party pays return shipping, and (d) any items that can't be returned (oversized freight, special-order). Same answers shape the Refunds messaging at checkout.

About Us page — Parsons writes or Apollo drafts?

The home page already has the About section linked. The full page can be (a) Parsons-supplied copy on company history and capabilities, or (b) an Apollo draft Parsons reviews and edits. Either way, two-day turnaround once the choice is made.

Cross-site links — which Parsons companies' sites should link to Shopautoequip, and vice versa?

The kickoff brief flagged this as a cross-site ecosystem play. We need the list of sister-company sites that should outbound to Shopautoequip (and which should receive inbound links from the Brands section). Apollo handles the placement once we have the list.

Free-shipping callout on the homepage — yes or no?

Since shipping is baked into MAP pricing, a "Free shipping included" callout on the home page can be a real conversion lift. Or it can be left off if Parsons would rather keep the messaging quieter. Apollo's recommendation is to include it.

04After launch — future enhancements

Capabilities to add in week 2–4 and beyond, once orders are flowing.
Customer reviews + ratings on product pages

Reviews lift conversion 10–30% on equipment categories. BigCommerce has native reviews; Yotpo or Judge.me add richer widgets and email solicitation. Best added once early orders have shipped to seed the reviews.

Email marketing platform (Klaviyo or Mailchimp)

Captures email signups, runs welcome series, post-purchase flows, win-back campaigns. Klaviyo is the e-commerce default. Worth standing up in week 2–4 once there's a list to mail.

Schema markup + rich Google results

Product schema unlocks Google Shopping rich results (price, availability, ratings in search). BigCommerce themes generate basic schema; can be extended with FAQ, Breadcrumb, and Organization schema for additional SERP real estate.

ShipStation for fulfillment + label printing

Once order volume is past ~10–20 per week, batch label printing and rate-shopping across carriers via ShipStation saves real time and money. Free for low volume, tiered after that.

Sage 100 Contractor integration

The original kickoff conversation flagged a bi-directional API between BigCommerce and Sage 100 Contractor for inventory and order sync. Worth revisiting once order volume justifies the integration cost — too heavy for launch.

Promotions + coupon system

First-purchase discount, free-shipping threshold, seasonal codes. BigCommerce native, no app needed. Add when there's a marketing reason to run one.

Live chat or chatbot for sales support

High-ticket equipment buyers ask pre-purchase questions. Tidio, Gorgias, or Intercom are common picks. Only worth it if Parsons can respond within minutes during business hours — otherwise it hurts more than it helps.
