01What we need from Parsons

Decisions and inputs to unblock Apollo's launch work.
Payment processor decision + underwriting
Blocker

Today the store is in test-card mode only — no live processor is connected. The store cannot accept a real order until this is plumbed in.

What we need: Parsons picks BigCommerce Payments (recommended — bundles cards, PayPal, Venmo, Pay Later, digital wallets in one checkout, no separate contracts) or Stripe (better if Parsons already has a Stripe relationship). Underwriting paperwork sits with the business owner — bank info, EIN, business address.

Sales tax direction
Blocker

Zero tax rates are configured today. Selling auto equipment across state lines triggers nexus rules — getting this wrong creates legal exposure and reconciliation work at year-end.

What we need: either (a) the list of states where Parsons has confirmed sales-tax nexus and Apollo configures manually, or (b) a green light to install Avalara AvaTax, which auto-applies the right rate per shipping address (monthly cost based on transaction volume).

Order notification recipient + customer-facing "from" email
Blocker

When an order comes in, someone at Parsons needs to be alerted so fulfillment begins. Customer-facing confirmation emails need a real "from" address tied to the domain so they don't look like spam.

What we need: the email of the team member who owns order fulfillment (admin alert recipient), and confirmation that orders@shopautoequip.com works as the customer-facing "from" address.

02What Apollo will do before launch

Tasks Apollo owns and will complete — no client interpretation required.
Configure multi-warehouse free shipping
Blocker

Per the documented requirement, shipping is included in MAP pricing and items ship from multiple warehouses. Apollo will add each fulfillment warehouse in Settings → Locations, enable the "Free shipping" method on the U.S. zone, and confirm product-to-location mapping reflects which warehouse each SKU ships from.

Connect the chosen payment processor
Blocker

Once Parsons completes underwriting on the chosen processor, Apollo configures the connection, enables live processing, and turns off test-card mode.

Set up tax (manual rates or Avalara)
Blocker

Once Parsons gives the direction, Apollo either configures the manual nexus-state rates in Basic Tax or installs and configures Avalara AvaTax.

Plumb in follow-up emails (order, shipping, abandoned cart)
Pre-launch

Apollo sets the admin alert recipient, points the customer "from" address at the domain, brand-passes the order-confirmation and shipping-confirmation templates, and turns on BigCommerce's native abandoned-cart saver (recovers 10–15% of abandoned carts on average).

End-to-end test order
Pre-launch

Once payment, shipping, tax, and follow-up emails are configured, Apollo places a real $1 test order using a Parsons card to verify the full flow: order placed, admin email received, customer email received, tax + shipping applied correctly. Then refunds. Any config issue surfaces here, not on launch day.

03Open questions we need answered

Quick decisions Parsons makes that unblock the Apollo tasks above.

Which payment processor — BigCommerce Payments or Stripe?

BC Payments is the simpler all-in-one (cards, PayPal, Venmo, Pay Later, wallets, no separate contracts). Stripe is more granular and worth choosing if Parsons already has a Stripe relationship.

Manual sales-tax setup or Avalara?

If Parsons has only one or two states of nexus, manual rates in Basic Tax are free and fine. If the catalog will sell into many states or scale fast, Avalara handles it automatically — there's a monthly cost based on transaction volume.

Returns policy specifics — return window, restocking fees, who pays return shipping?

These answers shape the Refunds messaging at checkout and any Return Policy page text. Apollo can draft starter language, but we need Parsons' actual practice on (a) return window in days, (b) restocking fees, (c) who pays return shipping, (d) any items that can't be returned (oversized freight, special-order).

04After launch — future enhancements

Capabilities to add in week 2–4 and beyond, once orders are flowing.
Customer reviews + ratings on product pages

Reviews lift conversion 10–30% on equipment categories. BigCommerce has native reviews; Yotpo or Judge.me add richer widgets and email solicitation. Best added once early orders have shipped to seed the reviews.

Email marketing platform (Klaviyo or Mailchimp)

Captures email signups, runs welcome series, post-purchase flows, win-back campaigns. Klaviyo is the e-commerce default. Worth standing up in week 2–4 once there's a list to mail.

Schema markup + rich Google results

Product schema unlocks Google Shopping rich results (price, availability, ratings in search). BigCommerce themes generate basic schema; can be extended with FAQ, Breadcrumb, and Organization schema for additional SERP real estate.

ShipStation for fulfillment + label printing

Once order volume is past ~10–20 per week, batch label printing and rate-shopping across carriers via ShipStation saves real time and money. Free for low volume, tiered after that.

Sage 100 Contractor integration

The original kickoff conversation flagged a bi-directional API between BigCommerce and Sage 100 Contractor for inventory and order sync. Worth revisiting once order volume justifies the integration cost — too heavy for launch.

Promotions + coupon system

First-purchase discount, free-shipping threshold, seasonal codes. BigCommerce native, no app needed. Add when there's a marketing reason to run one.

Live chat or chatbot for sales support

High-ticket equipment buyers ask pre-purchase questions. Tidio, Gorgias, or Intercom are common picks. Only worth it if Parsons can respond within minutes during business hours — otherwise it hurts more than it helps.
