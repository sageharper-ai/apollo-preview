03Open questions

Decisions still unresolved that block specific tasks above.

Avalara or manual tax rates?

Direction is decided (destination-based). Implementation choice depends on transaction volume forecast. If Parsons expects to sell into 5+ states regularly, Avalara handles it automatically (monthly cost based on volume). If concentrated in 2–3 states, manual configuration in Basic Tax is free and sufficient. Apollo recommendation comes after payment processor connects and we see early order distribution.

Returns policy specifics — timeframe, restocking fees, who pays return shipping?

Tom's input needed on (a) return window in days, (b) any restocking fees (typical for heavy equipment), (c) who pays return shipping, (d) any items that can't be returned (oversized freight, special-order). These shape the Returns page text and refunds messaging at checkout.

Chatbot platform — Apollo recommendation pending

Decision to add a chatbot is made. Platform choice is open: Tidio (cheapest, BC-native integration), Drift (sales-led), Intercom (full-featured but pricey), or BigCommerce native chat. Apollo to recommend based on (a) Parsons' inbound volume expectation and (b) who will respond when the bot escalates.

Email-marketing platform — needed pre-launch or post?

Klaviyo / Mailchimp would handle: welcome series, post-purchase flows, abandoned-cart enrichment beyond BC's native saver, win-back campaigns. Question: does Parsons want a richer email-marketing platform live at launch, or stand it up after first orders are flowing? Apollo recommendation: defer to week 2–4 unless Tom wants email capture from day one.

04After launch

Capabilities to add once orders are flowing.
100% review-cycle strategy (compete with Derek Weaver)

Sage flagged in meeting: post-launch, every order should solicit a review. Reviews lift conversion 10–30% on equipment categories and close the trust gap with established competitors. Yotpo / Judge.me / BC native — pick after launch volume settles.

Klaviyo or Mailchimp (if not stood up pre-launch)

Welcome series, segmented post-purchase flows, win-back campaigns. Klaviyo is the e-commerce default. Stand up week 2–4 once there's a list to mail.

Promotions + coupon system

First-purchase discount, free-shipping threshold (already baked into MAP, but stack offers possible), seasonal codes. BigCommerce native, no app needed. Add when there's a marketing reason.

ShipStation for label printing + multi-carrier rate shopping

Once order volume passes ~10–20 per week, batch label printing via ShipStation saves real time. Free for low volume.

Sage 100 Contractor integration

Original kickoff brief flagged a bi-directional API between BigCommerce and Sage 100 Contractor for inventory and order sync. Worth revisiting once order volume justifies the integration cost — too heavy for launch.
